EstateHat Business Plan

Operating plan for a trust-first real estate workflow platform

This business-plan summary is intended for early investor and strategic-partner review. It reflects the current public product posture in the EstateHat repository and frames how the company expects to scale software revenue and transaction coordination capabilities.

Executive summary

EstateHat is building a software platform for peer-to-peer real estate transactions. The product focuses on reducing workflow fragmentation, improving trust signals, and giving non-expert users a more guided operating surface across readiness, documents, messaging, and closing coordination.

The business is being framed around software coordination revenue, trust-first workflow design, and a measured expansion path that prioritizes compliance, reporting quality, and product credibility before aggressive growth spending.

Problem and thesis

  • Residential transaction workflows remain fragmented across too many tools and handoffs.
  • Trust, compliance steps, and process confusion can slow conversion and increase user error.
  • A unified, guided, verification-aware platform can reduce friction and improve execution quality.

Current product posture

  • Live web product at estatehat.web.app.
  • Public company, FAQ, legal, and investor pages.
  • Authenticated app shell with listing, profile, messaging, and transaction surfaces.
  • Native wrappers maintained for iOS, Android, and Windows packaging paths.

Target user posture

  • Users who want clearer transaction guidance, stronger trust signals, and fewer disconnected handoffs.
  • Buyers and sellers who value workflow clarity over a purely listing-centric experience.
  • Professionals and partners who can operate inside clearer readiness and coordination boundaries.

Core differentiation

  • Verification-aware product framing.
  • Role separation and trust language embedded in the public narrative.
  • One-surface workflow spanning discovery, messaging, forms, and closing readiness.

Business model

Revenue stream Current model Role in plan
Platform fee 1.50% of completed transaction sale price Primary revenue driver tied to closed transaction volume
Verified User subscription $20 per month Recurring trust and readiness revenue layer
Escrow and wire amounts $500 escrow and $35 wire shown in product flows Treated cautiously in planning because some or all may be pass-through to partners
EstateHat is intentionally treating software revenue as the central performance lens. Pass-through partner costs and any ambiguous fee retention should not be relied on as core revenue until they are operationally confirmed.

Go-to-market posture

  • Build credibility through trust-first positioning rather than broad undifferentiated marketplace spend.
  • Use the public site, legal posture, and guided product narrative to convert early users who value process clarity.
  • Expand GTM only after compliance hardening, reporting cadence, and live billing/payout integrations are in place.

Operating priorities

  • Complete live payout and billing integrations.
  • Strengthen KPI and investor reporting cadence.
  • Formalize compliance posture for broader market expansion.
  • Continue UX and trust-funnel conversion improvements.

Near-term financing use

  • Compliance and legal hardening.
  • Core integration work for payments and operational tooling.
  • Measured GTM testing and conversion optimization.
  • Security, reporting, and operational maturity.

Key risks investors should understand

  • Current public materials describe the product model and planning posture, not audited operating history.
  • Compliance, jurisdiction, and partner-integration execution remain important gating items for scale.
  • Transaction-growth outcomes depend on funnel performance, product trust, and measured GTM discipline rather than simple traffic acquisition.
