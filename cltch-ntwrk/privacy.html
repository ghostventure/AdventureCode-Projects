Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 8, 2026

Your privacy matters to us because trust matters to this platform. This Privacy Policy explains what information CLTCH.NTWRK collects, why we collect it, who can see parts of it, how we protect it, and what choices you have. We honor relevant U.S. laws such as the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) guidance where it applies, and related state privacy laws like Colorado’s CPA, Virginia’s VCDPA, and Connecticut’s CPA aligned with DMCA notice requirements.

1. What We Collect

We collect information you provide directly, information created by your use of the platform, and limited account-security information generated by the service.

  • Account information such as name, email address, CLTCH OneID, sign-in method, and basic account status.
  • Host and performer profile information such as venue details, stage or display name, location, genres, instruments, experience, rates, bios, and payout preferences.
  • Optional feature enrollment information such as Verified Performer status, subscription plan metadata, and activation/request timestamps.
  • Booking and workflow information such as gig posts, accepted gigs, passes, reviews, calendar-related booking state, and queue activity.
  • Security and account activity markers such as recent sign-in, sign-out, and password-reset activity used to help maintain account integrity.
  • Support and contact information you submit when asking for help.

2. Why We Use It

  • To create and maintain your account and profile.
  • To match hosts and performers based on availability, role, style, location, and booking state.
  • To operate gig posting, booking, review, and workspace features.
  • To support account security, verification, abuse prevention, and session protection.
  • To improve reliability, troubleshoot product issues, and respond to support requests.

3. What Other Users Can See

CLTCH.NTWRK is not a fully public social network. Most profile and booking visibility is limited to signed-in users inside the product.

  • Performer-facing profile details such as display name, genres, skill summary, location, reviews, and availability-related booking context may be visible to signed-in users where relevant to bookings.
  • If a performer enables optional verification features, a verification badge or status indicator may be visible to other signed-in users.
  • Host-facing information such as venue name, booking location, and gig details may be visible to performers when a gig is relevant to them.
  • Your email address is not displayed publicly as part of your profile.
  • Sensitive operational data is intended to stay inside authorized product flows, not public search indexing.

4. What We Do Not Sell

We do not sell your personal information to data brokers. We do not turn your account details, booking history, or profile information into an open public data feed. If we ever introduce third-party monetization or advertising practices that materially change this, we will update this policy.

5. Payments, Subscriptions, and Financial Information

Users may provide payout or payment method information inside the platform. The live product may prioritize certain payment paths before others. Some future payment-related features may appear in the interface or codebase before they are active in production. Unless CLTCH.NTWRK expressly states otherwise, we do not treat inactive or future-facing payment code as a live promise of processing services.

CLTCH.NTWRK may also offer optional paid profile features, including Verified Performer. If enabled, related plan details (such as monthly amount, activation timestamp, and active/inactive status) may be stored with your account and used to display verification indicators to other users.

6. Authentication, Verification, and Session Protection

CLTCH.NTWRK uses account verification, sign-in state checks, access controls, and inactivity handling to protect user sessions. Password accounts may be required to verify the email address on file before protected access is granted. We may store limited authentication activity markers and sign users out after inactivity to reduce the risk of abandoned or exposed sessions.

7. Matching and Calendar Data

The platform uses profile details, availability, role, and booking state to decide which gigs a performer can see and which performers may match to a host's post. Some booking-related data is stored in lightweight calendar or queue form to keep the site performant and usable. That data is used to prevent conflicts, drive matching, and support booking screens.

8. Service Providers and Infrastructure

CLTCH.NTWRK uses third-party infrastructure providers to run the service. The current platform uses Google Firebase for core authentication and data storage. If you sign in with Google, the sign-in flow is handled through Google's systems. We may also use other service providers for hosting, communications, and product operations as the platform grows.

We only share the minimum data necessary with those providers, such as authentication metadata, profile information for matching, communication data for notifications, and limited financial or payout details (subject to GLBA-style safeguards when financial data is entrusted to processors). All service providers must agree to handle our data under our privacy policy, and we require them to support compliance with CCPA, CPRA, and similar statutes.

9. Data Retention

We keep account, profile, and booking data for as long as needed to operate the service, maintain trust and security, and comply with legal or operational obligations. Some data may remain in backup, logs, or security records for a period of time even after a user-facing deletion request is processed.

10. Security Measures

We use technical and operational measures designed to protect user data, including authenticated access flows, verification checks, protected database rules, HTTPS delivery, session controls, and other safeguards. No service can promise absolute security, but we do work to reduce unnecessary exposure and to prevent unauthorized access where practical.

11. Your Choices and Requests

  • You can update much of your profile and notification settings directly inside the product.
  • You may request access to, correction of, deletion of, or a portable copy of your data through the contact form or by emailing privacy@cltch.ntwrk. We aim to acknowledge requests within 10 business days and complete them within 45 days, subject to legal retention obligations.
  • You can opt out of marketing emails, limit non-essential cookies (through our cookie settings), and disable in-product trackers that are not required for security or core functionality.
  • We do not discriminate against anyone for exercising these rights.

12. Children’s Privacy

CLTCH.NTWRK is not intended for anyone under 18. We do not knowingly allow minors to maintain accounts on the platform, and we delete any underage account once we learn of it unless parental consent and applicable local child labor protections are satisfied. This helps us comply with COPPA, FLSA child labor protections, and any relevant city, county, borough, or parish ordinances that limit the employment or participation of minors.

13. California Privacy Rights

California residents have the right to request disclosure of the categories of personal information collected, the sources, the business purpose, and the third parties with whom we share information. They also have the right to request deletion of personal information, correct inaccurate data, and opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information (we currently do not sell or share personal data beyond what the law permits). Requests may be submitted via privacy@cltch.ntwrk or through the contact page.

We do not discriminate if you exercise any of these rights, and we strive to acknowledge requests within 10 business days and fulfill them within 45 days unless a valid extension is necessary.

14. Additional State Privacy Rights

Residents of Colorado, Connecticut, Virginia, Utah, and other states with comprehensive privacy laws enjoy similar rights around access, deletion, correction, portability, and opting out of processing. We apply the same procedures and timelines (acknowledgement within 10 business days, completion within 45 days) to these requests so these rights are treated consistently.

15. Changes to This Policy

We may revise this Privacy Policy as the product evolves. If we make material changes, we will update the effective date on this page and may provide additional notice where appropriate. Continued use of CLTCH.NTWRK after the revised policy is posted means you accept the updated policy.

16. Contact

If you have privacy questions, want to request deletion, or believe your data has been handled incorrectly, contact us through the platform contact path or email privacy@cltch.ntwrk. We aim to respond to privacy inquiries within 45 days.
