DMCA Policy

CLTCH.NTWRK respects intellectual property rights and expects users of the platform to do the same. We comply with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. § 512) and maintain a registered agent for copyright notices. If you believe content hosted or displayed through the platform infringes your copyrighted work, you may send a DMCA-compliant notice as described below.

Notice Requirements (17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3))

  • Your physical or electronic signature (including an email confirmation) showing you are the copyright owner or an authorized agent.
  • A clear description of the copyrighted work you believe is infringed.
  • The precise location (URL or other identifier) of the allegedly infringing material on CLTCH.NTWRK.
  • Your contact information (email, telephone, postal address) so we can reach you.
  • A statement that you have a good-faith belief the use is unauthorized.
  • A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

Send your notice via email (preferred) or postal mail to the designated agent listed below. We begin processing notices within five business days and will remove or disable access to the material while we evaluate the claim.

Counter Notices (17 U.S.C. § 512(g)(3))

If you believe material was removed or disabled by mistake, submit a counter notice that includes your signature, identification of the removed material and its location before removal, a statement under penalty of perjury that the material was removed due to mistake or misidentification, and consent to federal or North Carolina state court jurisdiction. We will forward the counter notice to the original complainant and may restore the material after ten business days unless the complainant informs us it has filed a lawsuit.

Repeat Infringer Policy

Consistent with 17 U.S.C. § 512(i)(1)(A), we terminate accounts of repeat infringers and may disable access to any user who gathers multiple valid notices in a short period. We record every effective notice and rely on repeat-danger signals to keep infringing material offline.

False Notices

Knowingly submitting a false takedown notice or counter notice exposes you to liability under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f). Please use this process responsibly and only request removals when you are confident the content infringes your copyright.
