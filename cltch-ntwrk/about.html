About CLTCH.NTWRK

The network built for live music.

CLTCH.NTWRK connects hosts and venues with the musicians and DJs they need — on demand, with zero middleman friction. Post a gig, get matched, get paid. That's it.

Why we built this

Booking live talent has always been a manual, disconnected process — cold DMs, outdated referral lists, and missed opportunities. Performers spend more time chasing leads than performing. Hosts spend more time on hold than on the floor.

We built CLTCH.NTWRK to fix that. A real-time platform where gig postings surface instantly to matched, available performers nearby — and where a handshake happens in seconds, not days.

How it works

For Hosts

1

Create a venue profile

Add your venue details, contact info, and how you pay talent. Takes two minutes.

2

Post a gig

Specify the date, time, genre, pay rate, and what kind of performer you need. We geocode your location automatically.

3

Get matched and confirmed

Performers nearby who play the genre you need see your gig instantly. The first to accept locks it in. You get notified immediately.

4

Rate the performance

After the gig, leave a grade and short feedback. Ratings help great performers get more opportunities.

For Performers

1

Build your performer profile

Add your name, genres, instruments or setup, rate, and bio. Set your zip code so the matching engine can find gigs near you.

2

See gigs matched to you

Open requests that match your genre and location appear in your feed in real time. No scrolling through noise.

3

Swipe or tap to accept

Accept a gig in one tap. The date is automatically marked on your calendar so you stay organized.

4

Build your reputation

Every gig you complete earns a grade from the host. Your grade history is visible on your public profile.

What we handle vs. what you handle

✅

CLTCH.NTWRK handles

  • Location-based matching
  • Real-time gig feed
  • Availability calendars
  • Performer ratings & grades
  • Profile visibility
  • Booking confirmation
🤝

You handle directly

  • Payment between host and performer
  • Contracts or agreements
  • Equipment and logistics
  • Permits and licensing
  • Any post-booking communication
